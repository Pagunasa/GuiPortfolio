About me

Hi, I'm Gui! A game programmer, and designer from Barcelona. Since my childhood, I've loved the world of video games, always wanting to learn how to bring my ideas to life. I started experimenting with Gamemaker at the age of 14 and created my first video game at 17 as a final project for high school. It was a very simple Legend of Zelda-like game with a top-down camera.

I graduated from UPF BSM, where I studied the Master in Videogames: Design, Creation, and Programming, and from UPF where I studied Computer Engineering. On this website, you'll be able to see some of the projects that I've had the oportunity to work on, as well as personal work for experimentation and fun.

I have a strong perseverance and motivation to learn and improve, my vivid imagination often brings me some crazy ideas. I always polish my work to achieve the best possible version.

If you have any questions fell free to contact me

Remember Meow

Software used Unreal engine 5
Languages used C++, Blueprints
Primary Roles Gameplay programming, VFX
Remember Meow is the result of the master's degree project in video games at UPF BSM. In this project, the team consisted of 9 members: 3 artists and 6 programmers. My primary tasks were to design and implement engaging game mechanics, create useful tools for the team, work on the VFX, and optimize the game. Thanks to this project I achieve a more realistic and professional approach to video game design.
Download the game from our Itch.io page!
In Remember Meow, you play as a cat doll in a strange house infested with evil spirits. You can't remember anything of yourself, but a strange voice guides you around the house. Each time a puzzle is solved, you obtain a memory and advance through the house.

Dixie

Software used Sublime Text, Chrome
Languages used HTML5, CSS, JS
Primary Roles Design, coding
Dixie is the result of my final degree project for my bachelor's in Computer Engineering at UPF, an online graph-oriented particle motor.

Dixie allows you to create and export particle systems by customizing and connecting nodes. You can add forces, conditions, or modifiers for various particle properties such as speed, color, size, and lifetime.

AI Custom Engine

Software used C++ Custom Engine
Languages used C++, LUA
Primary Roles Design, coding
This is an exercise I completed during the master of video games at UPF BSM. I designed an enemy with the logic to rotate around the player while maintaining a certain distance. To make it more interesting, I imagine these enemies moving in hordes. For this reason, I use a shared blackboard to keep track of the number of enemies in attack mode and manage a maximum number, as well as to assign roles between wandering, moving, and attacking. If you scroll down a bit, you'll find the diagram illustrating its behavior tree.

Cardgeon

Software used LÖVE
Languages used LUA
Primary Roles Design, coding
Cardgeon is a deckbuilding game that is the final project of the course CS50's Introduction to Game Development, Video Games, and Programming. It is fully programmed in Lua using the framework LÖVE and takes inspiration from games such as Slay the Spire.

The main mechanic is to advance between rooms of a dungeon filled with monsters, managing your mana for cast cards. Every time a room is cleared you'll choose a new card and advance to the next room. Eventually, you'll find the final boss, and if you can kill it, you'll win the game, but if you die you'll have to start a new run.

Neera's Mission

Software used C++ Custom Engine
Languages used C++
Primary Roles Design, coding
Neera's Mission is the final project of the Electronic Games course at UPF. It is fully programmed in C++ using a custom framework developed and refined during the course.

It is a first-person, turn-based game in which the main mechanic involves exploring rooms of a dark spaceship and repairing generators while engaging in battles with aliens that have taken control of the ship. Once all generators in a room are fixed, a door will open, granting access to a new area. The primary goal is to repair the ship's main generator and successfully escape.
